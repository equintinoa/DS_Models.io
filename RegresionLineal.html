Caso Bidimensional del Modelo¶

Con el objetivo de entender mejor las justificaciones del modelo, comenzaremos por abordar un caso sencillo de bajas dimensiones y posteriormente generalizaremos las ideas. Supongamos que, dadas dos observaciones de una variable de entrada, queremos predecir los valores de una variable objetivo. Usando el modelo de regresión lineal, esto se representa como:

$$ \begin{cases} \hat{y}_1=\hat{\beta}_1 x_1+\hat{\beta}_2 \\ \hat{y}_2=\hat{\beta}_1 x_2+\hat{\beta}_2 \end{cases} $$

Para futuros cálculos, es conveniente representar al modelo en forma matricial; por lo tanto definimos las siguientes matrices

$$ \boldsymbol{X}=\begin{bmatrix} x_{11} & x_{12} \\ x_{21} & x_{22} \end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix} x_1 & 1 \\ x_2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} ; \quad \boldsymbol{\hat{Y}}=\begin{bmatrix} \hat{y}_1 \\ \hat{y}_2 \end{bmatrix} ; \quad \boldsymbol{\hat{\beta}}=\begin{bmatrix} \hat{\beta}_1 \\ \hat{\beta}_2 \end{bmatrix} $$

De esta manera, el modelo se representa como:

$$ \boldsymbol{\hat{Y}} = \boldsymbol{X} \boldsymbol{\hat{\beta}} $$

Detalle

$$ \begin{aligned} \left[\begin{array}{l} \hat{y}_1 \\ \hat{y}_2 \end{array}\right] &= \left[\begin{array}{ll} 1 & x_1 \\ 1 & x_2 \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l} \hat{\beta}_1 \\ \hat{\beta}_2 \end{array}\right] \\ &= \left[\begin{array}{l} \hat{\beta}_1 x_1 +\hat{\beta}_2 \\ \hat{\beta}_1 x_2 + \hat{\beta}_2 \end{array}\right] \end{aligned} $$

Optimización del Modelo¶

La pregunta que surge naturalmente es: ¿Cómo podemos escoger los coeficientes adecuados $\beta _1$ y $\beta _2$? Para esto definimos la función “Suma de los Cuadrados Residuales” como $$ \begin{aligned} \operatorname{RSS}(\boldsymbol{\hat{\beta}}) & =\left(y_{1}-\hat{y}_{1}\right)^{2}+\left(y_{2}-\hat{y}_{2}\right)^{2}=\sum_{i=1}^{2}\left(y_{i}-\hat{y}_{i}\right)^{2} \\ \end{aligned} $$

Esta medida de error puede representarse de forma matricial como

$$ \operatorname{RSS}(\boldsymbol{\hat{\beta}})= \left( \boldsymbol{Y} - \boldsymbol{X} \boldsymbol{\hat{\beta}}\right)^{T} \left( \boldsymbol{Y} - \boldsymbol{X} \boldsymbol{\hat{\beta}} \right) $$

Detalle

$\begin{aligned}\left( \boldsymbol{Y} - \boldsymbol{X} \boldsymbol{\hat{\beta}}\right)^{T} \left( \boldsymbol{Y} - \boldsymbol{X} \boldsymbol{\hat{\beta}} \right) & =\left[\begin{array}{c}y_1-\left(\hat{\beta}_1 x_n+\hat{\beta}_2 x_{12}\right) \\ y_2-\left(\hat{\beta}_1 x_{21}+\hat{\beta}_2 x_{22}\right)\end{array}\right]^{\top}\left[\begin{array}{c}y_1-\left(\hat{\beta}_1 x_n+\hat{\beta}_2 x_{12}\right) \\ y_2-\left(\hat{\beta}_1 x_{21}+\hat{\beta}_2 x_{22}\right)\end{array}\right] \\ & =\left(y_1-\left(\hat{\beta}_1 x_{11}+\hat{\beta}_2 x_{12}\right)\right)^2+\left(y_2-\left(\hat{\beta}_1 x_{21}+\beta_2 x_{22}\right)^2\right) \\ & =\left(y_1-\hat{y}_1\right)^2+\left(y_2-\hat{y}_2\right)^2\end{aligned}$

Para encontrar los mejores valores en los parámetros, diferenciamos lo anterior

$$ \begin{aligned} \frac{\partial}{\partial \hat{\beta}_1} \operatorname{RSS}(\boldsymbol{\hat{\beta}}) & =-2\left(y_1-\left(\hat{\beta}_1 x_{11}+\hat{\beta}_2 x_{12}\right)\right) x_{11}-2\left(y_2-\left(\hat{\beta}_1 x_{21}+\hat{\beta}_2 x_{22}\right)\right) x_{21} \\ & =-2 \sum_{i=1}^2\left(y_i-\sum_{j=1}^2 \hat{\beta}_j x_{i j}\right) x_{i 1} \\ \frac{\partial}{\partial \hat{\beta}_2} \operatorname{RSS}(\boldsymbol{\hat{\beta}}) & =-2\left(y_1-\left(\hat{\beta}_1 x_{11}+\hat{\beta}_2 x_{12}\right)\right) x_{12}-2\left(y_2-\left(\hat{\beta}_1 x_{21}+\hat{\beta}_2 x_{22}\right)\right) x_{22} \\ & =-2 \sum_{i=1}^2\left(y_i-\sum_{j=1}^2 x_{i j} \hat{\beta}_j\right) x_{i 2} \end{aligned} $$

De esta forma se tiene

$$ \frac{\partial}{\partial \boldsymbol{\hat{\beta}}} \operatorname{RSS}(\boldsymbol{\hat{\beta}})=\left[\begin{array}{l} -2 \sum_{i=1}^2\left(y_i-\sum_{j=1}^2 x_{i j} \hat{\beta}_j\right) x_{i 1} \\ -2 \sum_{i=1}^2\left(y_i-\sum_{j=1}^2 x_{i j} \hat{\beta}_j\right) x_{i 2} \end{array}\right] $$

Lo cual se puede representar como

$$ \frac{\partial}{\partial \boldsymbol{\hat{\beta}}} \operatorname{RSS}(\boldsymbol{\hat{\beta}}) = -2 \boldsymbol{X}^{T} \left( \boldsymbol{Y} - \boldsymbol{X \hat{\beta}} \right) $$

Detalle

$$ \begin{aligned} -2 \boldsymbol{X}^{T} \left( \boldsymbol{Y} - \boldsymbol{X \hat{\beta}} \right) & =-2\left[\begin{array}{ll} x_{11} & x_{21} \\ x_{12} & x_{22} \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l} y_1-\left(\hat{\beta}_1 x_{11}+\hat{\beta}_2 x_{12}\right) \\ y_2-\left(\hat{\beta}_2 x_{21}+\hat{\beta}_2 x_{22}\right) \end{array}\right] \\ & =-2\left[\begin{array}{l} \left(y_1-\left(\hat{\beta}_1 x_n+\hat{\beta}_2 x_{12}\right)\right) x_{11}+\left(y_2-\left(\hat{\beta}_1 x_{21}+\hat{\beta}_2 x_{22}\right)\right) x_{21} \\ \left(y_2-\left(\hat{\beta}_1 x_{11}+\hat{\beta}_2 x_{12}\right)\right) x_{12}+\left(y_2-\left(\hat{\beta}_1 x_{21}+\beta_2 x_{22}\right)\right) x_{22} \end{array}\right] \end{aligned} $$

Tenemos la siguiente ecuación normal, la misma tiene solución si $\boldsymbol{X^{T} X}$ es no singular. $$ 2 \boldsymbol{X}^{T} \left( \boldsymbol{Y} - \boldsymbol{X \hat{\beta}} \right)= \boldsymbol{0} \Rightarrow \boldsymbol{ \hat{\beta}} = \left( \boldsymbol{X}^{T} \boldsymbol{X} \right)^{-1} \boldsymbol{X}^{T} \boldsymbol{Y} $$

Generalización del modelo¶

Para obtener una generalización del modelo, supongamos que tenemos $n$ observaciones de $m$ variables. Así tenemos las siguientes matrices.

$$ \boldsymbol{Y} =\left[\begin{array}{c} y_1 \\ y_2 \\ \vdots \\ y_n \end{array}\right] ; \quad \boldsymbol{X}=\left[\begin{array}{cccc} x_{11} & x_{12} & \cdots & x_{1 m} \\ x_{21} & x_{22} & \cdots & x_{2 m} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ x_{n 1} & x_{m 2} & \cdots & x_{n m} \end{array}\right] ; \quad \boldsymbol{{\beta}}=\left[\begin{array}{c} {\beta}_1 \\ {\beta}_2 \\ \vdots \\ {\beta}_m \end{array}\right] $$

Sin pérdida de generalidad, supongamos que $x_{1m}, x_{2m}, \dots, x_{nm}$ son iguales a $1$. De esta forma, el modelo se representa como

$$ \boldsymbol{Y} = \boldsymbol{X \beta} $$

Suma de los cuadrados residuales $\operatorname{RSS}$¶

Para generalizar la funcion $\operatorname{RSS}(\hat{\boldsymbol{\beta}})$ se tiene

$$ \begin{aligned} \operatorname{RSS}(\boldsymbol{\hat{\beta}}) & =\left(y_1-\hat{y}_1\right)^2+\left(y_2-\hat{y}_2\right)^2+\cdots+\left(y_n-\hat{y}_n\right)^2 \\ & =\sum_{k=1}^n\left(y_k-\hat{y}_k\right)^2 \\ & =\sum_{k=1}^n\left(y_k-\sum_{i=1}^m \hat{\beta}_i x_{k i}\right)^2 \\ & =\left( \boldsymbol{Y} - \boldsymbol{X} \boldsymbol{\hat{\beta}}\right)^{T} \left( \boldsymbol{Y} - \boldsymbol{X} \boldsymbol{\hat{\beta}} \right) \end{aligned} $$

Al derivar cada término de la suma anterior obtenemos

$$ -2\left[y_k-\left(\hat{\beta}_1 x_{k n}+\hat{\beta}_2 x_{k 2}+\cdots+\hat{\beta}_j x_{k j}+\cdots+\beta_m x_{k m}\right)\right] x_{k j}=-2\left[y_k-\sum_{i=1} ^{m} \beta_i x_{k i}\right] x_{k j} $$

Por lo tanto, la derivada de la función $\operatorname{RSS}(\boldsymbol{\hat{\beta}})$ es

$$ \begin{aligned} & \quad \frac{\partial}{\partial \hat{\beta}_j} \operatorname{RSS}(\boldsymbol{\hat{\beta}})=-2 \sum_{k=1}^n\left(y_k-\sum_{i=1}^m \hat{\beta}_i x_{k i}\right) x_{k j} \\ & \Rightarrow \frac{\partial}{\partial \boldsymbol{\hat{\beta}}} \operatorname{RSS}(\hat{\beta})=-2\left[\begin{array}{c} \sum_{k=1}^n\left(y_k-\sum_{i=1}^m \hat{\beta}_i x_{k i}\right) x_{k 1} \\ \sum_{k=1}^n\left(y_k-\sum_{i=1}^m \hat{\beta}_i x_{k i}\right) x_{k 2} \\ \vdots \\ \sum_{k=1}^n\left(y_k-\sum \hat{\beta}_i x_{k i}\right) x_{k m} \end{array}\right] \\ & \therefore \frac{\partial}{\partial \boldsymbol{\hat{\beta}}} \operatorname{RSS}(\hat{\beta}) =-2 \boldsymbol{X}^{T} \left( \boldsymbol{Y} - \boldsymbol{X \hat{\beta}} \right) \end{aligned} $$

Si $\boldsymbol{X^{T} X}$ es no singular, la siguiente ecuación tiene solución. $$ 2 \boldsymbol{X}^{T} \left( \boldsymbol{Y} - \boldsymbol{X \hat{\beta}} \right)= \boldsymbol{0} \Rightarrow \boldsymbol{ \hat{\beta}} = \left( \boldsymbol{X}^{T} \boldsymbol{X} \right)^{-1} \boldsymbol{X}^{T} \boldsymbol{Y} $$

def coef_LinnearModel(X, Y):

    X = np.c_[X, np.ones(X.shape[0])]
    X_t = np.transpose(X)
    beta = np.matmul(np.matmul(np.linalg.inv(np.matmul(X_t, X)), X_t), Y)
    
    return beta

def linear_regression(X, beta):
    X = np.c_[X, np.ones(X.shape[0])]
    Y_pred = np.matmul(X, beta)
    return Y_pred
